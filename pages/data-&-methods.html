Data and Methods

Understand the methodologies used in data collection, validation, mapping, and archival processes.

View Methodology

1. Research Design

This study employed a descriptive and developmental research design. The descriptive component focused on gathering and organizing existing data related to sea turtle nesting activities in selected coastal areas of Antique. The developmental component involved the creation of a website-based archiving and mapping system to present the collected data in an accessible and ethical manner.

2. Data Sources

Data used in this study were obtained from publicly available secondary sources to ensure ethical handling of sensitive wildlife information.

2.1. News and Media Reports

Local and national news articles were used to identify reported sea turtle nesting events, hatchling releases, and conservation activities. These sources provided general location information, species identification, and year of occurrence.

2.2. Government and Institutional Publications

Publications and advisories from local government units and environmental organizations were used to support and validate reported nesting activities within the study area.

Academic Literature

Peer-reviewed research papers and conservation studies published between 2011–2025 were referenced to identify sea turtle species present in Antique and to provide baseline and supporting scientific context.

3. Data Selection and Inclusion Criteria

Only data that met the following criteria were included:

  • Related to sea turtle nesting or hatchling events
  • Located within San Jose, Antique or nearby coastal areas
  • Provided non-exact location information
  • Published by credible news, government, or academic sources
